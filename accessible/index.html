Philosopher · Educator

King's College London  ·  London, UK

I am a PhD candidate in Philosophy at King's College London. My primary research sits at the intersection of philosophy of mind, phenomenology, and epistemology, driven by the question: How do I understand the Other? My doctoral thesis develops a new reading of Edith Stein's theory of empathy (Einfühlung), arguing that we can know another person's emotions directly — and non-perceptually — through empathic knowledge.

Alongside my doctoral work, I have a research interest in early modern philosophy, particularly the epistemological and social thought of the Third Earl of Shaftesbury.
